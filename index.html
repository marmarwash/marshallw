Hello! I am a senior Undergraduate Researcher in the Modern Languages, Literatures, and Languages department at the University of Oklahoma. I will receive two B.A.’s in linguistics and Chinese with honors in May 2025. My senior thesis examines classifier analyses in Mandarin, specifically seeking to synthesize knowledge from Chinese linguists and linguists who study classifier typology more broadly.

Currently, my research interests include studying gender neutral pronoun creation and usage across the world’s languages, specifically within the 2SLGBTQ+ community. These projects have been funded by OU’s Undergraduate Research and Creative Activities (UReCA) Fellowship, as well as the McNair Scholars program. As an incoming M.A student at the University of Colorado Boulder, I hope to examine gender neutral pronoun analysis within LLMs, and anti-queer hate speech moderation using AI.

I also have a background in anthropology, studying biological anthropology within the Kemp Working Group in the Laboratories of Molecular Anthropology and Microbiome Research (LMAMR). My projects focused on analyzing ancient fish DNA in order to better understand trade and migration patterns of Chinese Immigrants to the United States during the 1800’s. In another project funded my UReCA, I conducted an online ethnography that sought to understand Reality Shifting and how the internet facilitates innovative perceptions of selfhood.

In my free time, I like to crochet, travel, and share my thoughts on Substack. After studying Mandarin and Vietnamese, I’m currently trying to learn Indonesian!
